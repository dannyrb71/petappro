# Prior Competitive Analysis — Danny, Fall 2024 (class project)

**Source:** `assets/DBaker_Rover_Competitive_Analysis_Fall2024.pdf` (14 pp). Rover-anchored study; competitors evaluated *against* Rover.
**Why it's here:** it predates the PetAppro competitive thread and is **already experience-first**. We adopt its rubric and carry forward its evidence; it is a reference asset, not the current core set.

**Apps analyzed:** Direct — **Wag!, Care.com, PetBacker**. Indirect — **PetSmart (PetHotel), Wag Hotels**.
**Framework used per app:** purpose · founded · revenue streams · funding · downloads/traffic · categories · social/content · personalization · community/UGC · competitive advantages · region · **heuristic evaluation** · customer reviews · SWOT.

## Rubric to adopt (this is the valuable part)

Danny scored each app's experience on four heuristics, with concrete friction notes:

- **Easy to Understand** — is the purpose/taxonomy clear?
- **Easy to Navigate** — can you move through setup/booking without confusion?
- **Consistency** — does app match web/brand; do patterns hold?
- **Helpful Feedback** — does the app guide you, show value, and respond to actions?

→ Folded into `_template.md` as the standard experience rubric.

## Carry-over insights (relevant to PetAppro)

**Onboarding — show value before commitment.** Repeated friction across apps: forced to pay an application/background-check fee or create an account *before* seeing the experience. Danny's note: would stop and leave. PetBacker (which let you experience the site pre-registration) scored best on this. → PetAppro onboarding (invite/QR) should still reveal value early, not front-load friction.

**Trust = pet-profile depth.** PetSmart's minimal pet profile made the pet feel "like just another SKU"; Wag Hotels asked for *no* pet details (bad). Apps that asked rich pet questions felt more caring/trustworthy. → Reinforces rich pet profiles + care notes as a trust lever.

**Anti-marketplace evidence (feeds D-029/D-026):**
- PetBacker takes **25–30% commission**; Wag runs **~2× Rover's fees**.
- Care.com's model is "confusing — listing service or marketplace?"; pet care feels like an afterthought behind childcare/senior care.
- PetBacker pays providers directly, so sitters must run their own Venmo/Zelle/CashApp — the exact informal-payments problem PetAppro productizes away.
- Marketplace apps split logins/accounts confusingly (Wag's two apps; Care can't reuse login) — a warning for our client/staff context model.

**UX friction patterns to avoid:** duplicate/ambiguous Save buttons (Save vs. Next/Submit); web pages framed inside the app feeling oversized; "back" logging you out; unclear which of two apps is for which user type.

**Marketing/adjacent (hand to Marketing track, not product):** social-channel observations — TikTok-first strategies, per-platform content quality, paid-search gaps. Wag Hotels had the strongest curated social presence; several drove little traffic from social.
